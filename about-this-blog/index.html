About This Blog

Why

Originally I was trying to say things like my goals are to explain things simpler for the others while also learning from it. Well, it definitely goes beyond that. It's not just one goal that propels myself to open a blog, just like you have tons of factors that you would take into account when you decide whom you marry to.

Though I do really want to make things simpler, not just it feels good, but also

  • it is indeed simple underneath (derived from various steps)
  • there were too much garbage on the Internet >
  • Paid Courses
  • No fucking context, just concepts
  • Either too fucking abstract, or too fucking not serious (examples that some could not get)
  • Code that could not withstand the changes (thus outdated)
  • anything could be learned

After all, it was generated by your fellow humans, right?

What

I have a dream that you might consider weird, that is, read every books in this world. It, of course, could not ever be achieved, either logically or just in plain common sense.

Even though I could not do that, I still want to consume and understand as much as possible for the materials that interested me.

And that, could not be achieved if I just save a bunch of links to my bookmark services, and maybe another tens of thousands of books on my computer, like a data hoarder we are increasing become (or already are, lol), and many other types of media, digitally or physically.

Currently I'm holding hold thirty-ish read-later articles. Though I still consider it as too much, my strategy for tackling them are like this: put them in real-world use, converting them into a content-dense blog post and so on.

Exhaustion & Realization

Sometimes I think that there were already tons of people doing the X Concept Simplified, and even doing a way better job that me (I haven't even getting started, so it's not a fair comparison for them, lol. Props to them!). So why the fuck I'm getting out of this during the process?

Then I realized that, or just telling myself a cloying narrative, it's all about the process, and more importantly, about me. It is me who gets to understand the concepts, and also do something about it with my own context (aka. words that related to experiences like childhood, environment, family, education, basically, all of the different things combined that makes you YOU).

Credibility

I would include resources that seemed the most accurate, as I do not have unlimited resources of time, mental energy. Just like what StackOverflow (or I should say all the sub-sites under StackExchange) really is, from my own experience for the answers I've seen (please know that the actual thing I'm critiquing on is the endless abstractions which programmers fell into and the lack of strong and accurate foundational knowledge).

By no means I have the authority to critique about any of the sites I mentioned here. I was, after all, offering my own prejudices for you to read on.
