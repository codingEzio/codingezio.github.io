## Intro

### Why

> As you and I all know, even for a normal *Spring* project, it's already complex enough for the human brain to comprehend just **in one go** <small>(with or without a good documentation)</small>. Just like it would be unimaginably more complex for projects like *Google Play Store* that would need to provide various APIs, payment stuff and many other details <small>(I was going to choose Twitter back then, then I realized that it wasn't really looking *that* complicated **for** a non-technical person. Anyway, just think of it as a really complicated project, and a growing one!)</small>.
>
> For me, I think the reason for companies like Google asking interview questions that related to *System Design*, is to see if the person really has the ability to have a clear grasp and an organized view for the projects she/he/x gonna built, and also, you would need to *anticipate for the future*. As the project grows, you **have** to consider [scaling resources like *Database*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database_scalability#Hardware) and concepts like [Database *Sharding*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shard_(database_architecture)).
