Consume Less

Posted on November 06, 2019

Or should I say taking other people's advice

幾個月前, I saw a post called 'consume less, create more' yes, the whole concept is great (and so does the whole thing to himself)

but, that doesn't apply to everyone, (p.s. I'm not here to judge the author)

instead, I think we should be

  • more cautious about taking advice
  • to put more clearly, consume less social media
    • Twitter, Reddit indeed is a great place to go
    • you can get almost anything there (more narrowly, advice & info)
    • but in the end, it is their life (I'll elaborate on this down below)

11 11 = 121 9 9 + 4 4 + 4 6 = 121 this might be a weird example, what I'm trying to say here is that just it looks (hmm)similar(huh?), that doesn't mean you should follow it, you might think "I never do this before, so it won't affect me!". NO. the stuff you consume also influence your views on the topics you're interested, therefore altered your decisions, sometimes it's big, sometimes it's not-a-big-deal.

alright, enough of the crap, the only actual thing I wanna underline is:

  • highlight the prerequisites
  • state your current state/status/mood in comparison with the stuff/post you write
  • update periodically if you can (it would be great if you also use version control)
  • ...

you might not want to show your personal data, so I'd suggest put it in a more abstract one (which is completely doable, I'm sorry I currently don't have a strong example now)
