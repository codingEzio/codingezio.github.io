荐

Working on real-world software projects, going to networking events, and studying your ass off just to stay current.

You

  • have to be amazing
  • have to be amazing to be able to create your own opportunities
  • have to be amazing to be able to create your own opportunities in order to have more control over your life

Find projects and contribute!

You look a whole lot better to potential employers if you can honestly tell them you've contributed meaningfully in real-world software projects, even if it was not necessarily in the context of a job.

Doing OSS contrib in home is like hack for interning

Even having a little bit of real-world experience is much preferred by employers over having no real-world experience.

思

In order for you to create valuable software (software actually used by humans), you must first know why it's valuable, who it affects, and what it's supposed to do. That involves communicating with people.

Nobody will go out of their way to provide you with career growth opportunities. It will be necessary for you to find and create your own opportunities, from the very beginning of your career to the very end.

Not sure what to learn?

  • Think about the kinds of software you want to build.
  • Search for that kind of software and learn about what tools and technologies are needed to build those kinds of applications.

From there, you have all the information you need to begin your self-guided education.
