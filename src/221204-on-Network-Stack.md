## Intro

### Why

> Back then, I remembered that I wondered about what exactly is the source code of *CSS* itself.
>
> Eventually I came to a understanding that there were only **specifications** <small>(like [this one](https://www.w3.org/TR/CSS2/))</small>, and actual implementation were all *up to the browsers* <small>(or [browser engines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_browser_engines) like [Blink](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blink_(browser_engine)) which browsers like *Chrome*, *Edge* all depended upon, to be more specific)</small> to implement.

### What

> I could, of course, read the thousands of [RFCs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Request_for_Comments) on [Internet Protocol Standards](https://www.rfc-editor.org/standards). Well, it's always good to read what the authors/implementers think or envision, but that stripped me from enjoying the fun of actually *running*, *testing* and maybe *breaking* them like I could do for a [Vue.js](https://vuejs.org/) front-end project.
>
> In order to achive that, I have to find a way to *implement* them and getting the hella fun out of it 😁.
>
> ***Current stage: finding good resources that could lead me to accomplish that.***
