## Note

> It’s funny that it has been two years or so from actually trying to break into the source code of the *self* inside me. Ever since the college years, I developed a systematic way to think while protecting the integral part of self, like always be kind to the others, be interested in everything and so on (weird context? Yep, felt the same to my current self as well. I just have to pour these stuff out before these memories went in the hibernation mode again).
>
> Of course, the major objective of developing the mental framework is to accomplish things, whether it’s as big as a goal, or as small as a simple decision in life like *shall I fulfill the need for high calorie by going to McDonald*. The old while it’s also the current way to do it, is simply by take advantage of the laziness which everyone has, to put it more clearly, it’s *I just want to go home now to have a rest instead of walking extra length to get that meal which is an unnecessary one*.

> Now it’s time to break all the mental framework and start again. It definitely did not help me much while reviewing the past few years.
>
> The changes would be applied right from this sentence. I shall try to improve the ways of doing things when I encountered one, instead of just reading some articles about self improvement then wanting to improve things like *when you have a hammer in your hand, everything looks a nail to you* (this is bad and not effective way to do it).

> Sometimes, I think it’s quite sad that any blog posts, to the deepest level, would only be written for the author herself/himself as different humans just have too many different contexts from each other.
