### On Team *Size*

> "Organizations." Organizational design gets the right people in the right places, empowers them to make decisions, and then holds them accountable for their results. Maintained consistently and changed sparingly, nothing else will help you scale more.
>
> Team should be six to eight at steady state. To big, there would be no time to learn more about the problem domain the team’s facing/tackling with or simply any other high-level work. To small, they are not exactly A team but INDIVIDUALs. The disadvantage is mostly centered around the fragility, for things like on-call or a sudden pandemic, it will NOT adapt well.
>
> To create a new team, grow the existing team to eight or ten members, then bud them into two teams of four to five (e.g. conquer and divide 🤭).
