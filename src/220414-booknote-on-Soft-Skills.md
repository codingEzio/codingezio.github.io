### Notes

#### 荐

> The best way to learn something is to jump right in and start doing, before you even know what you’re doing. If you can gain enough knowledge about a subject to start playing around, you can tap into the powerful creative and curious nature of your own mind.
>
> 👉 Start creating/playing as early as possible.
>
> Once you’ve played around (learn by doing) and you have all kinds of questions, only then go back and read the text. Now when you go back to your reference material, you’ll approach it with a greater desire to devour and absorb its contents. You’ll have questions that you’ll want answers to; you’ll have an idea of what’s important.

#### 效

> Before you read a book on a subject, skim the book and dive right in and start playing around. Don’t worry if you don’t know what you’re doing. Just have fun and see what kind of questions your mind develops as you experiment and explore.

#### 习

> Three main things I needed to know to be able to learn a technology
>
> *how to get started* (e.g. FP for Erlang)
>
> - the basic things that I need to know to get started
>
> *the breadth of the subject*
>
> - how is the thing I'm learning
> - an overview of (you can always find more details later)
> - what I could do
> - what is possible
>
> *(beyond) the basics*
>
> - what were the most common things I need to know to use it (context, ecosystem)
> - what were the basic use cases
> - what the 20% info that I should know to cover 80% of the situations.

> Start by getting a basic understanding of what you’re trying to learn—enough to know what you don’t know. Then take that information and use it to define the scope of what you want to learn, along with what success will look like.
>
> - to know WHAY YOU DON'T KNOW
> - to know WHAT YOU WANNA KNOW

#### 缩

> Clearly define exactly what I needed to learn and to look for the best resources I could find to get exactly the information I needed while ignoring any additional information that wasn’t required to meet my goal.

> Forces you to clearly define a goal for what you’re trying to learn upfront, and it constantly moves you in the direction of that goal by actually doing instead of just reading or listening to lectures.
