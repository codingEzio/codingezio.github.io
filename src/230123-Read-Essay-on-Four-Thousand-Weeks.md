## Streamed Thoughts

4000 weeks for a human life (around 76 years)
yet 1 week feels so limited (time set in stone, yet more tasks)

productivity technique does work
but you are almost never be able to get everything under control, e.g.
- flood of emails contained
- todo-lists that stopped getting longer
- all the obligations at work being met
- perfect work-life balance being achieved
- fully optimized how you could be

ah, this is quite depressing, isn't it?

no, do not take this more than a different perspective, and yes, it's
better you could leave your emotions behind for the previous list :)
even for me, I couldn't quiet my mind during the time of writing this
post.

first, embrace 'limited time, infinite tasks, leave behind some'
stop and consider things, rationally, and confront the limitations

tough choices need to be made
- which people to disappoint
- which cherish ambitions to abandon
- which roles to fail at

and yes,
you would miss out on almost EVERY experience that this world has to offer 
you would miss out on A LARGE PART of things you wanna do yet has no time

now, they are almost equivalent
why don't you just try to focus on the real important/essential things

now, that's a decision
not a trigger for stressful emotions or deadlines

## Tips

### two todo list

> however the result is, you are indeed working on something you wanted to

- one is nightmarishly long
- one has a fixed amount of things to do, like a `Queue` with capacity `5`

### strategic underachievement

- nomiate in advanced whose ares of life you won't expect excellence from

### device

- put distractigng apps as far as possible (not easy to reach)
- make your phone boring like turn on *grayscale* mode
- try single-purpose devices like an e-reader (low spec like refresh rate)

### novelty in the mundane

- instead of cramming novelty, pay twice the attention to the exp you have

### cultive instantaneous generosity

- to give money, to check in on a friend, to praise, to help family etc.
- act on it right way, the **only** acts of gen is **the one you're making**

### practice do-nothing

- for me, the reaction of *I need to do something!!* arises when I'm anxious
- a timer
  - sit 
  - and do nothing 
  - and think nothing
  - once you notice you're doing/thinking something, *stop*
  - are you critizing yourself for it? *stop* as well
  - timer goes off
